The problem

Activity ≠ outcomes.

The hospital chains you compete with have full-stack marketing teams, performance dashboards, and a video studio inside the building. You don't. The patient searches happening in your catchment right now don't care about that. They're going to whichever hospital shows up first, ranks first, and has the most recent reviews. That's where the gap opens.

You show up later than competitors

Three other hospitals appear above you on "‘[your specialty] near me’." Half your catchment never scrolls past them.

The existing database goes cold

Five thousand patients who've walked through your hospital, never re-engaged. Easiest revenue you'll find. Sitting unused.

Reviews build trust. You don't have enough.

The hospital with 40 Google reviews loses to the one with 200, every single time. Reviews don't accrue by accident.

Why six months

Why 180 days, not 60.

A 60-day engagement only proves the rented channels: paid, WhatsApp, Google Business. Turn those off, the leads stop.

The compounding layers (SEO content, doctor brand, review velocity, referral-doctor relationships) take months to build the trust signals that lower your cost of acquisition.

A 180-day engagement gives the compounding layers time to ship and start working alongside the rented ones. That's the gap.

How the two layers work together

  • Paid + WhatsApp + GMB. Rented attention. Fast revenue. Turn off, leads stop.
  • SEO content. Compounds at month 4–6. Earns you traffic the chains pay for.
  • Doctor brand. Months of consistent shorts and stories to earn followers, saves, DMs.
  • Review velocity. A review count that catchment patients actually trust accrues over months, not weeks.
  • Referral doctors. GP and specialist relationships take quarters to build, not days.
  • Branded search. The compounding layers create demand the engine then captures.

Questions, answered

What hospital owners actually ask.

Why six months and not a 30 or 60-day pilot?
A 60-day engagement only proves the rented channels: paid, WhatsApp, Google Business. The compounding layers (SEO, doctor brand, reviews, referral network) take months to build the trust signals that lower your cost of acquisition. Six months gives both layers time to ship and start working together. If you want to exit earlier, the Day 30, 60, and 90 gates are there.
Will you guarantee a specific lift in OPD enquiries?
No, and we think any agency that does is over-claiming. Marketing outcomes depend on catchment, seasonality, doctor availability, and competitor activity. What we will commit to: every deliverable on the published schedule, transparent monthly reporting, and the four exit gates. If we miss a phase deliverable, the following month's fee is waived.
Is this for a chain or a single hospital?
Single-unit standalone hospitals between 50 and 300 beds, typically ₹3–30 Cr in annual revenue. Owner-doctor or family-led. Usually one specialty leading the OPD. If you're a chain with three or more units, our offer is different. Please write to info@qlarify.health.
Is ad spend included?
No, ad spend is pass-through and billed separately on actuals. Typically ₹50,000–1,00,000/month in Phase 1, scaling to ₹1,50,000–2,50,000/month by Phase 3. We size it to your catchment and patient lifetime value on the diagnostic call so you can model payback before you commit.
How much time do you need from our doctor?
Four hours total across 180 days. One on-site shoot day (around 3 hours) plus a 30-minute monthly review. We don't need the doctor on every video, every paid creative, or every campaign. Just enough to make the brand recognisably theirs.
Will you call me to pitch after the audit?
No. You'll get the report. If you want to talk, you book the call. If you don't, you get one polite check-in a week later. That's it. No multi-touch nurture sequence, no LinkedIn add-and-DM.
What happens to my data?
Stays with us. Used only to deliver the audit and follow up once. Not shared, not resold, not added to a third-party marketing list. DPDP compliant.
